Quantum computing in simple terms:

  • Regular computers use bits (0s and 1s)
  • Quantum computers use quantum bits or "qubits"
  • Qubits can be 0, 1, or both simultaneously (superposition)
  • This allows quantum computers to process vast amounts of possibilities at once
  • They excel at specific problems like encryption, modeling molecules, and optimization

Think of it like this: If solving a maze, a regular computer checks one path at a time, while a quantum computer explores all paths simultaneously.
